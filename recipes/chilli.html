Spicy Vegan Chili


Description

Spicy Vegan Chili is a robust and flavorful dish that combines a variety of vegetables and spices to create a hearty meal. This recipe features a mix of bell peppers, onions, and jalapeño peppers sautéed with garlic and seasoned with chili powder, cumin, and salt. The addition of crushed tomatoes, black beans, and corn provides a rich texture and depth of flavor, while the optional jalapeños add a spicy kick. This chili is perfect for those who enjoy a bit of heat in their meals.

The preparation involves sautéing the vegetables and spices, then simmering them with tomatoes, beans, and rice until everything is tender and well-blended. The final touch is a sprinkle of vegan cheese, which adds a creamy finish to the dish. This chili is ideal for a cozy dinner, game day, or any occasion where you want a satisfying and nutritious meal. Serve it with cornbread or over rice for a complete and delicious vegan meal.


Ingredients

  • 1 tablespoon canola oil
  • 1 large green bell pepper, diced
  • 1 large onion, diced
  • 2 jalapeño peppers, chopped (optional)
  • ¼ cup chili powder
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 ½ teaspoons salt
  • ½ teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 (28 ounce) can crushed tomatoes
  • 1 (15 ounce) can black beans, rinsed and drained
  • 1 ½ cups water, or as needed
  • 1 cup rinsed, uncooked white rice
  • 1 (15 ounce) can corn, drained
  • ½ teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 1 (8 ounce) package shredded mozzarella-style vegan cheese (optional)

Directions

  1. Heat oil in a pot over medium-high heat. Sauté bell pepper, onion, and jalapeño peppers in hot oil until tender, about 3 minutes. Add chili powder, garlic, salt, and cumin. Cook, stirring occasionally, for about 10 minutes, making sure chili powder does not burn.
  2. Stir in tomatoes, beans, water, and rice. Bring to a boil. Reduce heat to low, cover, and simmer until rice is fully cooked, about 20 minutes. Check after 10 to 15 minutes if extra water is needed; add 1/4 cup at a time.
  3. Stir in corn and black pepper, and cook until heated through, about 5 minutes. Serve with vegan cheese if desired. Enjoy!
